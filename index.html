Landscape design

Landscape design is the art and practice of arranging outdoor spaces for aesthetic, functional, and environmental purposes. It involves the planning, design, and implementation of various elements to create harmonious and visually appealing outdoor environments. Here are some key aspects of landscape design:

Site Analysis: Before beginning the design process, landscape designers conduct a thorough analysis of the site, taking into account factors such as climate, soil type, topography, existing vegetation, drainage patterns, and sunlight exposure. This information helps inform design decisions and ensures that the design is tailored to the specific site conditions.

Garden installation

Garden installation is the process of physically implementing the design created during the landscape design phase. It involves transforming the conceptual plans and ideas into a tangible outdoor space by installing various elements such as plants, hardscape features, and other landscape elements. Here's a detailed explanation of the garden installation process:

Preparation: Before beginning the installation process, the site needs to be prepared. This may involve clearing the area of debris, removing existing vegetation or structures, grading the land to ensure proper drainage, and preparing the soil for planting.
